[SOFT] Custom circuit board shape for Fritzing in Inkscape

tl;dr: A short explanation of how to make your own board shapes in Fritzing.

Convention badges, game controllers, mechanical keyboards: there are tons of projects out there requiring a circuit board that doesn't look like a plain rectangle.

Designing your board shape

  • Draw your shape.
  • Give your shape a color. Preferably make it easy on the eyes: when you work on a board, you're going to spend a long time looking at it! You can adjust the Fill color and the Opacity settings until you like it.
  • Create a sub-layer named "board".
  • Right-click on the "board" layer to duplicate it and rename it "silkscreen". This layer should be on top.
  • Click on the shape of your board in the "silkscreen" layer. In the Object menu, select Fill and Stroke. Make sure there is no fill color on this layer, only a stroke.

Setting up your board for Fritzing

  • Open the XML editor - Edit > XML Editor or Control + Shift + X.
  • Look for the lines with "inkscape:label=" followed by the name of your layers. These lines define the name of each layer in your design.
  • On the line for each layer, click on the name in "id" value to your right and change it to the name of your layer. For example, the board layer's original id might be "layer2": change this to "board", then click the "Set" button.

Saving for Fritzing

  • Go to File > Document Properties.
  • In the Custom size section , click on the Resize page to drawing or selection button.
  • Finally, click on File > Save As... to save your design as Plain SVG.

Importing your board in Fritzing

  • Start Fritzing and create a new project.
  • Click on the PCB tab at the top of the screen.
  • Drag a PCB on the main section of the screen or. If you already have a PCB there, just left-click on it.
  • In the Inspector to the bottom right of your screen, click on Load image file.
  • Browse to the SVG file you saved earlier, then click Open.
Inkscape will confirm loading your board, and warn you about some of its features. If everything looks right, congratulations! You designed your owb PCB shape. If not, go back a few steps to see where you made a mistake, or start over entirely.
